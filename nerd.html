Acquisition Humor

When I was living in Japan and studying Japanese, one of the weirdest parts of acquiring a new language was the style of joke that my classmates and I found funny.

There's this strange class of humor that is only funny to those in the process of acquiring a language. For example, when learning Spanish, the false cognate "embarazado" is hilarious, because it refers to male pregnancy, not to embarrassment â€“ though it hilariously tends to lead to an even more embarrassed man.

While this little nugget of humor is hilarious for a cohort of beginning language scholars, native speakers would react to our attempts at humor, because language is so integral to their day-to-day lives that the novelty is entirely null. I suspect that in English, it'd be something like a joke that went "Is there any cake LEFT?" "Yes, there is. I LEFT it in the fridge... to the LEFT of the eggs! AHA!" which is decidedly NOT even remotely funny. Even calling it a joke is a bit of a stretch.

In other words, I am fully aware that my "coding jokes" are another instance of what I've decided to call acquisition humor, but as I acquire them, I intend to add them here. The probably won't be funny, and they will probably betray my utter lack of experience, but I'll add them as I go.
